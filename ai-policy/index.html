01

Our Position

Artificial intelligence is part of the modern studio, and we are not closing our minds to new technology. We use digital tools every day, and we understand that the boundary between a "tool" and a "generator" can feel blurry.

But the music we represent has to be yours — composed, played, and recorded by a human being. That human authorship is exactly what keeps your work protectable, our catalog clean, and our clients confident. So for everything you deliver to Songs To Your Eyes, we are drawing a clear line.

02

Where We Draw The Line

What's Acceptable OK

Using AI the way you would use any other studio tool — to sketch an idea, find a reference, or get unstuck on a hook — is fine, as long as you compose, perform, and record the actual music yourself, and anything AI touched is only a tiny part of the finished piece. This is consistent with how the collecting societies treat creative tools.

What's Not Acceptable Prohibited

The line is crossed the moment AI does the actual creating. For anything you deliver to us, the following are not allowed:

  • Generative composition Creating melodies, chords, beats, arrangements, lyrics, or vocals with AI.
  • AI-produced parts Whole tracks, instruments, or vocal lines generated by tools rather than performed.
  • "Enhancing" with Suno / Udio & similar Running your human recordings through generative tools to upscale, finish, or "improve" them. This re-renders the audio as effectively 100% AI — it becomes unsuitable for DSPs and is flagged instantly by AI scanners.
03

Why This Matters

This isn't about taste — it's about keeping your work, and the whole catalog, legally and commercially safe:

Copyright & Personal Liability

Purely AI-generated material is not eligible for copyright protection, because it lacks the human authorship the law requires. Most AI platforms' terms of service place the legal risk on you in the event of an infringement claim, and some explicitly forbid commercial use or claim ownership of whatever their tools output.

Blocked Delivery & Royalty Clawbacks

Distributors now run automated AI detection. Flagged tracks can be blocked before they ever reach a streaming platform — and it goes further: some distributors are retroactively identifying AI tracks and clawing back royalties that have already been paid out.

Catalog-Wide Blacklisting

Sync, TV, and trailer agencies actively screen submissions with AI-detection software. A single flagged track can damage our reputation and, in the worst case, get an entire catalog — or the company that submitted it — permanently blacklisted. Major trailer houses have already said they will not accept AI content at all.

04

What We Ask Of You

Please deliver only music that you genuinely wrote and performed yourself.

If you used an AI tool anywhere in your process — even a small part — tell us before you deliver. We would always rather have an honest conversation up front and make the right call together than discover a problem after the music is out in the world. When in doubt, ask first.

Thank you for understanding, and for the real craftsmanship you bring to every delivery. It's exactly why we work with you.
